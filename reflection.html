Reflection: What Happens After One Decision?

This page invites you to think, not to feel guilty. If one cheap-clothing choice is repeated by millions, what kind of social and environmental outcomes does it create?

Questions for the Reader

  • If a T-shirt is extremely cheap, who or what might be paying the hidden cost?
  • How often do I buy clothes because I need them, and how often because they are on sale?
  • Would I buy less if I knew more about workers' wages and factory pollution?
  • What changes are realistic for me: fewer items, better quality, secondhand, or repairs?

Quick Impact Thought Experiment

Move the slider to estimate how many cheap T-shirts one person buys in a year.

Final Thought

The butterfly effect reminds us that small actions matter when they become habits. Better choices do not need to be perfect: buying less often, keeping clothes longer, or checking ethical labels can weaken throwaway culture.
