One Cheap T-Shirt, Many Ripple Effects

This website explores the butterfly effect behind one daily choice: buying a cheap T-shirt. The decision feels small because you save money. Yet that action connects to labor, waste, and environmental consequences far beyond the store.

The purpose of this site is to make those hidden links visible. It is not about blaming people with limited budgets. It is about asking what system is reinforced when cheap clothing becomes normal.

Ripple Chain Overview

1) Local (You)

A low price creates immediate savings and encourages frequent buying.

2) National (Waste)

Fast clothing turnover increases textile waste and landfill pressure.

3) International (Labor)

To keep prices low, suppliers face cost pressure that can suppress wages.

4) Environmental (Planet)

Textile production consumes water and chemicals that can damage ecosystems.

Why This Matters

The butterfly effect is connected cause and effect. One purchase is small, but repeated by millions, it supports a throwaway model with hidden social and environmental costs.

Learning this chain helps students discuss responsibility, fairness, and sustainable choices with stronger evidence instead of simple opinion.
